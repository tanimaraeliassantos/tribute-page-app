Mary Jackson

The first black woman to work at NASA

Here's a timeline of Mary Jackson's life:

  • 1921 Born in Hampton, Virginia.
  • 1942 Bachelor's degrees in mathematics and phtsicial science from Hampton University.
  • 1944 Married to Levi Jackson
  • 1951 Recruited by the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics
  • 1953 Started working with engineer Kazimierz Czarnecki in the Supersonic Pressure Tunnel
  • 1958 After taking graduate courses at then-segregated Hampton High School through court, Mary Jackson became the first black female engineer at NASA
  • 1979 Became the Program Manager for the Langley's Federal Women's Program taking a demotion from her position. She worked hard to impact the hiring and promotion of the next generation of NASA female mathematicians, engineers and scientists.
  • 1985 Retired from Langley
  • 2005 Died aged 83.
