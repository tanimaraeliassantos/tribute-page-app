Mary Jackson

The first black woman to work at NASA

Here's a timeline of Mary Jackson's life:

  • 1921 - Born in Hampton, Virginia.
  • 1942 - Bachelor's degrees in mathematics and phtsicial science from Hampton University.
  • 1944 - Married to Levi Jackson
  • 1951 - Recruited by the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics
  • 1953 - Started working with engineer Kazimierz Czarnecki in the Supersonic Pressure Tunnel
  • 1958 - After taking graduate courses at then-segregated Hampton High School through court, Mary Jackson became the first black female engineer at NASA
  • 1979 - Became the Program Manager for the Langley's Federal Women's Program taking a demotion from her position. She worked to impact the hiring and promotion of the next generation of NASA female mathematicians, engineers and scientists.
  • 1976 - Awarded Langley's Volunteer of the Year. Langley is a Research Center that works to develop technology for space exploration.
  • 1985 - Retired from Langley
  • 2005 - Died aged 83.

"I plan on being an engineer at NASA, but I can't do that without taking them classes at that all-white high school, and I can't change the color of my skin. So I have no choice, but to be the first, which I can't do without you, sir. Your Honor, out of all the cases you gon' hear today, which one is gon' matter hundred years from now? Which one is gon' make you the first?"

-- Mary Jackson
